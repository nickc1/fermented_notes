## Swamp Rose

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/rosa-palustris/

Rosa palustris, commonly called swamp rose, is an upright deciduous shrub with arching branches that typically grows to 3-6' tall and as wide. It is native to wet ground (swamps, marshes, ditches, stream banks) from Nova Scotia to Ontario to Minnesota south to Florida and Louisiana.


![Swamp Rose](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Rosa_palustris--Carol-Foil--CC-BY-NC.jpg)

## Plantain-leafed Sedge

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/carex-plantaginea/

Tolerates wet planting sites and presents a striking pleated texture complementing shade and woodland native flowers.  This plant is shade tolerant, the leaves brown in the sun no matter how much water is dispersed.

![Swamp Rose](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Carex_plantaginea--Jay-Turner--CC-BY.jpg)

## Sabal Palmetto

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/sabal-palmetto/

Native tree in the palm (Arecaceae) family. It is not hardy in piedmont or mountains but does well in the southeastern tip of NC's coastal plain.  It is found in maritime forests, marsh edges, and other near-coastal communities.  This plant is highly salt tolerant.

NC Threatened species; found only in Brunswick Co., NC (and in states farther south).



![Sabal Palmetto](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/sabal_palmetto_katja_schultz_ccby20.jpg)


## Coral Honeysuckle

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/lonicera-sempervirens/

Lonicera sempervirens, commonly called Trumpet Honeysuckle, is a vigorous twining vine that is primarily native to the southeastern U.S. but has naturalized in many other areas of the eastern U.S.

![Coral Honeysuckle](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/lonicera__sempervire_WiCxR6cUsRYp.jpg)


## Bay Live Oak

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/quercus-virginiana/

Live Oak is a medium-sized showy long-lived evergreen tree that may grow 40 to 80 feet tall. It is found in the coastal plains of VA, NC and southward along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.


![Bay Live Oak](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Quercus_virginiana--_OfQKJ461OYhz.jpe)

## Black Eyed Susan

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/rudbeckia-fulgida/

Black-eyed Susan is an erect herbaceous perennial that may grow 2 to 3 feet tall. The many yellow daisy-like flowers with a brownish-purple center first mature in early summer and continue into the fall. A rosette of leaves that originate at the base of the stem persists through the winter, creating an attractive winter ground cover. Leave the seed heads on for the birds.


![Black Eyed Susan](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/BlackEyedSusan-LucyBradley_9427978643_resized.jpg)


## Sabal Minor

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/sabal-minor/

Sabal minor, commonly known as the dwarf palmetto, is a small species of palm growing 2'-7' in height. It is native to the deep southeastern and south-central United States and northeastern Mexico. Fruits are eaten by robins, raccoons, and many other birds and mammals.

![Sabal Minor](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Sabal_minor--Scott-Zona-CC-BY-NC.jpg)


## Butterfly Milkweed

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/asclepias-tuberosa/

Butterfly Weed is a tuberous rooted, native perennial that occurs in dry/rocky open woods, glades, prairies, fields, and roadsides. The plant is a larval host plant for monarch (which appear in the spring and summer and may have one to three broods in the north and four to six broods in the south), gray hairstreak, queen, and milkweed tussock moth caterpillars.

![Butterfly Milkweed](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/asclepias_tuberosa_wholeplant_Mary_Keim_CC_BY-NC-SA_2-0.jpg)


## Yaupon Holly

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/ilex-vomitoria/

Yaupon is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern USA and is primarily found in coastal NC. It can reach 10 to 20 feet tall and 8-12 feet wide.  Flowers appear in spring on separate male and female plants.


![Yaupon Holly](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Ilex_vomitoria_frfm__X0fVTDgNqnza.jpe)


## Wax Myrtle

https://plants.ces.ncsu.edu/plants/myrica-cerifera/

Waxmyrtle provides excellent winter and extreme weather cover. It is a host plant for the Red-Banded Hairstreak butterfly. Flowers provide an excellent source of nectar for honeybees and butterflies. The fruits are eaten by birds, especially yellow-rumped warbler (which are very efficient at digesting the waxy fruits) in the fall and winter.


![Wax Myrtle](https://s3.amazonaws.com/eit-planttoolbox-prod/media/images/Myrica_cerifera_fm2__qkee0oqC8yuo.jpeg)
